Fundación Manglar Vivo proposes a community-managed mangrove restoration program across four coastal communities in the Cartagena bay, directly advancing PVBLIC's Resilient Wealth & Nature Accounting (RWNA) framework. Over nine years and fourteen delivered projects, our locally-led model has restored 312 hectares of mangrove ecosystem while building measurable adaptive capacity.

Theory of Change from profile

By coupling community co-management with rigorous ecological monitoring, the program generates a reinforcing loop: restored mangroves reduce storm surge exposure, which protects livelihoods, which sustains community stewardship of the ecosystem over the long term.
